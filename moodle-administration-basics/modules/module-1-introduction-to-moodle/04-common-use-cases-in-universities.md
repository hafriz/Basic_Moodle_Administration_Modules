# Common Use Cases in Universities


Universities commonly use Moodle for:

- Publishing course outlines and weekly learning materials.
- Managing online assignments.
- Conducting quizzes, midterms, and practice tests.
- Supporting blended learning and flipped classroom models.
- Providing discussion forums for course communication.
- Tracking student participation and completion.
- Managing grades and feedback.
- Supporting continuing education and professional development programs.
- Integrating with authentication systems such as LDAP, SAML, OAuth2, or institutional single sign-on.
- Integrating with library systems, video tools, plagiarism detection services, and student information systems.

Example: A lecturer may upload weekly lecture slides, create an assignment submission activity, grade the submissions, and release feedback to students through Moodle. The IT support team may help when students cannot access the course or when file uploads fail.
